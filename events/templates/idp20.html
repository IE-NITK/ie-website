What is Industrial Defined Problem ?

IDP is short for Industrial Defined Problem and concerns a problem statement which is prevalent in a certain kind of industry. For instance, corrosion problem in pipelines for transporting oil and gas. These problems can either be extant or already been solved. Usually, such problems are given by the industries/ startups but, a professor or like can also give out the same provided they are affiliated to an industry somehow. The solutions to these problems can be accepted by the industry to solve the problem or it can be rejected. They do not rely on such solutions.

The Institution

National Institute of Technology Karnataka (NITK) is one of the premier engineering institutions in the country. Situated near Mangalore along the coast of the Arabian Sea, NITK is home to some of the brightest young minds of the country engaged in a wide array of disciplines. With a vibrant and robust student body, NITK has been the launchpad for people with great ideas that have grown to be very successful ventures including Practo, Perpule, SaveMonk among others. This year, we celebrate our Diamond Jubilee. To commemorate 60 years of NITK, we aim to gather all like minded hackers to our own institute.

FAQs


  • Who is eligible to apply?

    Students enrolled in any college in 2nd and 3rd years from any Dept are welcome to apply.

  • How many team members?

    3 members in a team are required to apply.

  • Is there any registration fee?

    No. Registrations are completely free of cost.

  • Will accommodation be provided?

    Unfortunately, we won't be able to provide any accommodation to students.

  • Is a working model required?

    No, a simulation will be sufficient (if required).

  • Can team members be from different branches?

    Yes, team members can be of different branches given all are in second or third year.

  • Can you be a part of multiple problem statements?

    Yes you can take up multiple problem statements in Prelims. Though if you qualify for the finals you can only take ONE problem statement.

  • Still got questions?

    Contact us using the information given below.

  • How many teams get selected for the finals?

    We are planning to select 6-8 teams across 4 problem statements for the final.
